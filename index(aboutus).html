About Us:

Company History

The easiest way to start is by telling the story of how your business was born. it works well when it took a while to happen, even if it is a recent brand. Start with the original founders and your reasons for starting the business.

This description should be under 150 words and get straight to the “why” of your business. What makes your company better than all others? This is your value, and it must be clearly stated.

If your story gets too long, break it down into important milestones, then imagine them in a row time. Too many key events? Choose milestones that interest your audience. For example, the date on which you joined the company may be interesting to you, but not to your audience.

Mission and Vision

If your product or service is “revolutionary”, it is critical that you include the mission or vision. use this opportunity to detail the problem your product or service solves. So share your expectations for the future of your company. Sharing the vision helps sell potential customers the idea behind a new product.

Testimonials

Customer testimonials can be very helpful in acquiring new customers. If you receive a compliment, say thank you and ask for permission to publish your testimonial.

No testimonials available? It costs nothing to order one. A great time to ask for a testimonial is when you speak directly with a customer who is happy with your product or service. Keep in mind that testimonials don't always have that come in the form of a traditional quote about your brand.

Positive testimonials reassure potential customers of your product or service.

Images

Use images to show customers what your business looks like. If you choose to focus on the team you runs the company's services, workspace or mascot, images can go a long way way towards giving customers a first look at what your brand looks like. Make sure to include a caption for each photo, so your audience clearly understands the message you want to convey with your images.

If you choose to use images as the main focus of your About Us page, remember that they should not hurt your company's narrative. Use them strategically to not only highlight what's important, but also to connect with the client.
